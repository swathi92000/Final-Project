Biryani Recipe

Step 1: Marinating the Chicken
1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the following:
  • Yogurt
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Garam masala
  • Salt
  • Lemon juice
2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and mix well.
3. Allow it to marinate for at least 30 minutes, preferably longer in the refrigerator.
Step 2: Preparing the Rice
1. Wash the Basmati rice thoroughly until the water runs clear.
2. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes.
3. In a large pot, bring water to a boil.
4. Add the soaked rice along with:
  • Green cardamom pods
  • Cloves
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Bay leaf
  • Salt
5. Cook the rice until it's 70-80% cooked (still slightly firm).
6. Drain the rice and set it aside.
Step 3: Frying Onions
1. Heat oil or ghee in a deep, heavy-bottomed pan or biryani pot.
2. Add thinly sliced onions and fry them until they turn golden brown and crispy.
3. Remove half of the fried onions and set them aside for garnish.
Step 4: Layering and Cooking
1. In the same pan, where you fried the onions, add the marinated chicken.
2. Cook on medium-high heat until the chicken changes color and is partially cooked.
3. Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies.
4. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the chicken is almost cooked through.
5. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture.
6. Sprinkle chopped mint and cilantro over the rice. Drizzle saffron milk and ghee (optional) over the top.
7. Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid and reduce the heat to low.
8. Cook for about 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is tender, and the rice is fully cooked.
9. Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork.
10. Garnish with the reserved fried onions.
Note: Feel free to adjust the spice levels and ingredients to suit your taste preferences. You can also explore variations like vegetable biryani or mutton biryani using similar methods.
